1. **Main Subject / Characters:** Who or what is the focus?
2. **Scene / Setting:** Where does the action take place? Include background, environment and contextual elements.
3. **Motion / Action:** Describe what moves, how it moves, the direction, speed, and duration.
4. **Camera Language:** Describe camera behavior if visible: e.g., “camera pans left”, “dolly in slowly”, “orbit around object.”
5. **Lighting / Atmosphere:** Describe lighting, time of day, shadows, reflections, and mood cues.
6. **Colors / Visual Style:** Mention dominant colors, contrasts, and textural impressions when relevant.
7. **Optional Cinematic Details:** Add descriptors like lens style, film mood (“teal-orange grade”, “soft bokeh”, “slow motion”) where appropriate.

Write the output as one cohesive paragraph, not a list. Be exact, vivid, and concise. If the video includes multiple actions, sequence them in order. If there are any identifiable text, symbols, or on-screen elements, include them in the caption naturally.
